LOCAL STORIES FROM PRESTON

Misty Mountains Cold: Imagining a New Landscape in Preston

By Bilbo J. Johnson

It has been a long time since the time I went to visit the hills outside Preston, Idaho. Probably as much as 60 years since, being a little boy, I got up with difficulty but with eagerness at 5 in the morning (in fact, it was my beloved grandmother who had the tremendous task of waking up that little me who was a gigantic opponent to rise up). My grandpa was every morning those summer waiting for me at the door of their small house in the countryside. He had already had his little breakfast probably fifteen minutes before I got up, and had already done 3 to 5 chose by the time I was finishing my first food in the morning (nothing too fancy at those first hours in the morning, just half fruit or one piece of bread). We would do that every morning.

As a big boy (or a young man), I still had time to think about how wonderful and magical my surroundings were. The small hills looked like the Everest, and I could think of those old stories my grandma used to tell me from the books of his youth (he was an avid reader of J.R.R. Tolkien, the famous writer of The Lord of The Rings Saga). Life was magical at those time, maybe because I was a bot, or maybe because my grandpa was able to bring Tolkien's Universe to a common place like Preston. It was the year 2025.
