A Little Hope for an Old Lady

By Clarissa M. Burkenhaimersen

Memories is all that is left to any old person in his or her last years. That is true for any person in the World, and Soda Springs elderly people are no exception. Marysa O'Sullivan Thompson is an octagenarian Irish inmigrant who went through two World Wars before coming to the United States on her fortieth decade. She was 15 years old at the end of World War 1, and 42 by the end of World War 2. She had a peculiar life regarding these historical events.

She was age eleven when the Archduke Franz Ferdinand was assassinated by one of her cousins! She was somewhat related to Gavrilo Princip, whom along with other five men was responsible for the detonating event that led to the subsequent war that ended up with the life of around 20 million people in five years (and 21 million wounded). At the time she was at Gavrilo's house! She and her family had to spend a couple of weeks on prison due to suspected involvement of her parents in the murder of the Archduke (it was uncommon to find any Irish man like her dad those days on Sarajevo). And the adventures of Mrs. O'Sullivan Johnson.

On the morning of December 7th, 1941, she was 500 yards from the first bomb that fell over Pearl Harbor, Hawaii.
