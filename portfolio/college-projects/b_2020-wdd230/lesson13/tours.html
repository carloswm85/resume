Brief Summary of the island

Cozumel (Spanish pronunciation: [kosuˈmel]) is an island and municipality in the Caribbean Sea off the eastern coast of Mexico's Yucatán Peninsula, opposite Playa del Carmen. It is separated from the mainland by Cozumel Channel and is close to the Yucatán Channel. The municipality is part of the state of Quintana Roo, Mexico.

The economy of Cozumel is based on tourism, with visitors able to benefit from the island's balnearios, scuba diving, and snorkeling. The main town on the island is San Miguel de Cozumel.

Best places to visit

  • Faro Celerain Eco Park (Punta Sur Eco Beach Park).
  • Laguna Chankanaab & the National Marine Park.
  • Cozumel's Mayan Heritage: San Gervasio
  • Celarain: Cozumel's Lighthouse

ATV Excursions

There is no better way to explore the dense jungles and beautiful but deserted beaches of Cozumel's East shore than on an ATV tour. The Eastern half of Cozumel is virtually uninhabited, and seldom visited. There is only one road, and that ends before you reach your destination! You'll board your ATV and ride through the wilds of Cozumel island, visiting Mayan Villages, snorkeling on remote coral reefs, and even seeing ancient Mayan Ruins and caves! These ATV tours are great cruise excursions too - only 2 to 4 hours in length, you can see the island and still have time to explore!
